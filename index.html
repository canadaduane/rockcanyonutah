Canyon History

Rock Canyon, adja­cent to Provo, Utah, draws thou­sands of peo­ple annu­ally who seek recre­ation, renewal, and inspi­ra­tion. It is one of the few remain­ing “URBAN FORESTS” in the United States which is eas­ily acces­si­ble and free for pub­lic use.

Vis­i­tors from all over the world are inspired by its MAJESTIC BEAUTY and UNIQUE GEOLOGICAL FEATURES.

What activ­i­ties do peo­ple enjoy in Rock Canyon?

Recreation

Hik­ing * Climb­ing * Moun­tain Bik­ing * Base Jump­ing * Pic­nick­ing * Snow-shoeing * Cross Coun­try Ski­ing * Sled­ding * Cou­ples’ Dates * Dog Walk­ing * Snow Board­ing * Horse­back Rid­ing * Rap­pelling * Ath­letic Train­ing * Spelunking

Education

Bird Watch­ing * Col­lege Field­trips in Geol­ogy, Art, Botany, Biol­ogy, Recre­ation Man­age­ment * Wilder­ness Train­ing Pro­grams * Insect Col­lect­ing * Study of Big Horn Sheep Pop­u­la­tions * Geo­log­i­cal Stud­ies * Wildlife Resource Man­age­ment Classes * Wild­flower Iden­ti­fi­ca­tion * Research Projects in the Nat­ural Sci­ences

Community

Fam­ily Walks * Preschool Out­ings * Ele­men­tary School Field Trips * Utah Native Plant Soci­ety Gar­den * Search & Res­cue Train­ing
